Who I am
A little about me

My path into public health started in a microbiology lab, and that foundation has never really left me. Understanding how pathogens behave, how infections spread, and how laboratory data is generated gives me a different lens when working on surveillance or disease monitoring.

Most recently I worked in childhood lead poisoning surveillance at the Massachusetts Department of Public Health, where my work touched everything from data analysis and visualization to coordinating with clinical and laboratory partners to strengthen reporting workflows.

I care about health equity in a way that goes beyond putting it on a resume. The questions that keep me engaged are about who gets missed, which communities carry disproportionate burden, and what the data looks like when you actually disaggregate it.

I'm based in San Jose and actively connecting with people working across public health, data, environmental science, and infection prevention.
